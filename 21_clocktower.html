Jubilee Clock Tower


The Jubilee Clock Tower, also known as the Queen Victoria Clock Tower, is an iconic clock tower situated at the junction of Lebuh Light (Light Street) and Jalan Tun Syed Sheh Barakbah in George Town, Penang. It was constructed in 1897 to commemorate Queen Victoria's Diamond Jubilee, marking her 60-year reign as the Queen of the United Kingdom.


The clock tower stands tall at a height of 60 feet and features a Moorish design, with a combination of Victorian and Islamic architectural elements. The tower is adorned with intricate details, including carvings, arches, and a copper dome. The clock itself was made in England and installed in the tower during its construction.


The Jubilee Clock Tower serves as a significant historical and cultural symbol in Penang. It not only commemorates Queen Victoria but also represents the colonial heritage of the city. The tower has become an iconic landmark and a popular spot for locals and tourists to gather and take photographs.


Surrounded by historical buildings and within walking distance of other attractions in George Town, such as Fort Cornwallis and the Penang State Museum, the Jubilee Clock Tower is an important point of interest in the city's UNESCO World Heritage Site. Its strategic location and distinctive architecture make it a recognizable and beloved feature of Penang's skyline.

Frequently Asked Questions


How to get to Jubilee Clock Tower?

To get to the Jubilee Clock Tower in George Town, Penang, you can walk from Komtar bus terminal in approximately 10-15 minutes or use a ride-sharing service or taxi to reach the tower directly. If you're driving, you can use GPS to navigate to Lebuh Light. Parking options may be available nearby.

Is the Jubilee Clock Tower accessible to the public?

Yes, the Jubilee Clock Tower is a public monument and can be accessed by visitors.

Can I go inside the Jubilee Clock Tower?

No, the interior of the Jubilee Clock Tower is not accessible to the public.

Are there any entrance fees to visit Jubilee Clock Tower?

No, there is no entrance fee to visit the Jubilee Clock Tower. It is open to the public free of charge.
