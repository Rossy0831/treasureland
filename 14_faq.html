Planning your trip


What are some basic facts about Penang?

Penang is a state in Malaysia located on the northwest coast. Its capital, George Town, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its history and culture. With a population of around 1.8 million, Penang has a diverse economy, including manufacturing and tourism. The local cuisine is famous and blends Malay, Chinese, and Indian flavors. Key attractions include Kek Lok Si Temple, Penang Hill, and Batu Ferringhi Beach. The state celebrates various festivals like Chinese New Year and Hari Raya Aidilfitri.

The best time to visit Penang, Malaysia, depends on personal preferences. The dry season from December to February offers pleasant weather but can be crowded and expensive. March to April and September to November have a mix of sunny and rainy days, fewer crowds, and better deals. The monsoon season from May to August brings heavy rainfall, but it's a good time for indoor activities and experiencing the local culture and cuisine. Overall, the dry season or shoulder seasons are recommended for a visit to Penang.

How can I get to Penang?

Penang is easily accessible by various modes of transportation. Here are some common ways to reach Penang:

By Air: The most convenient way to reach Penang is by flying into Penang International Airport (PEN). It is well-connected to major cities in Malaysia and several international destinations. From the airport, you can take a taxi, Grab (ride-hailing service), or airport shuttle bus to your desired destination in Penang.

By Road: If you are already in Malaysia or in a neighboring country, you can reach Penang by road. The North-South Expressway connects Penang to major cities in Malaysia, and you can drive or take a bus to reach the island. There are also cross-border buses from Singapore and Thailand that offer direct routes to Penang.

By Ferry: Another scenic way to reach Penang is by taking a ferry from the mainland. The ferry operates between Butterworth (near the city of George Town) and Penang Island. The journey takes about 15-20 minutes, and ferries run frequently throughout the day.

By Train: If you prefer traveling by train, you can take a train to Butterworth railway station, which is located on the mainland. From there, you can either take a ferry or a taxi to reach Penang Island.

Where should I go in Penang?

In Penang, there are several must-visit destinations. George Town offers historical and cultural experiences, while Penang Hill provides stunning views. Kek Lok Si Temple showcases impressive Buddhist architecture, and Clan Jetties offer insights into Chinese heritage. Batu Ferringhi is a popular beach area, and Penang National Park is great for nature enthusiasts. Exploring the vibrant street food scene is a must. Overall, Penang offers a mix of history, culture, nature, and culinary delights.

Do I need a passport or visa to enter the country?

To enter Malaysia, including Penang, you generally need a valid passport with at least six months of validity beyond your intended stay. The visa requirements depend on your nationality. Some countries have visa-free entry, allowing you to stay for a limited period without a visa. However, if your country is not eligible for visa-free entry, you may need to obtain a visa before traveling to Malaysia. It's important to check with the nearest Malaysian embassy or consulate or visit the official website of the Malaysian immigration authorities to determine the specific requirements based on your nationality.

Where are the heritage zones in Penang?

The heritage zones in Penang are primarily located in the capital city of George Town. These zones include the UNESCO World Heritage Site, which covers the historic core of George Town, including areas like Armenian Street, Muntri Street, Love Lane, and the Clan Jetties.

For more information, please visit the heritage zones page.

What are some history stories about Penang?

Penang has a rich historical background shaped by various significant events. It was established as a British trading post in 1786 and became part of the Straits Settlements. The island attracted immigrants from different cultures, resulting in diverse settlements like the Clan Jetties and Little India. Penang endured the Japanese occupation during World War II. It gained independence in 1957 and became part of Malaysia in 1963. The capital, George Town, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, preserving its historical and architectural treasures.

While In Penang


How can I get around in Penang?

Getting around in Penang is convenient with various transportation options available. The Rapid Penang bus system covers most areas, while taxis and ride-hailing services like Grab are easily accessible. Trishaws offer a nostalgic way to explore George Town. Renting a car or motorcycle provides flexibility, but walking is ideal for exploring nearby attractions. Plan for traffic congestion during peak hours. Public buses, trains, and private cars are available for intercity travel. Overall, Penang offers a well-developed transportation system catering to different needs and budgets.

The currency in Penang, Malaysia, is the Malaysian Ringgit (MYR). Cash is widely accepted, and there are ATMs available where you can withdraw local currency. Credit cards are generally accepted in hotels, larger restaurants, shopping malls, and tourist-oriented establishments. However, it's a good idea to carry some cash for smaller shops, street vendors, or places that may not accept cards.

Regarding tipping, it is not a common practice in Malaysia, including Penang. Tipping is not expected, but it is appreciated for exceptional service in upscale restaurants or for hotel staff who provide exceptional assistance. Some higher-end establishments may add a service charge to the bill, so it's worth checking before tipping. Overall, tipping is discretionary and not mandatory in Penang.

What should I dress in penang?

In Penang's tropical climate, it is recommended to wear lightweight and cool clothing. The weather is generally sunny and moderately hot, making it comfortable for outdoor activities. However, it's advisable to carry a light cover-up as shopping malls and restaurants are often air-conditioned. For formal occasions, men can wear western-style suits or batik shirts, but it's best to confirm the dress code with the event organizer. Modest attire is necessary when visiting places of worship, and mosques usually provide appropriate cover-ups for tourists. Remember to remove your shoes when entering religious sites.

It's recommended to carry a compact umbrella when engaging in outdoor activities in Penang, and it's important to stay hydrated by carrying a water bottle. However, it's important to note that drinking tap water directly is not advisable in Penang.

What languages are spoken in Penang?

While the official language of Malaysia is Malay, English is widely spoken by many locals in Penang. Additionally, most Penangites are proficient in more than two languages. One distinct language spoken among the Chinese community is "Penang Hokkien," which incorporates Malay and English words. A common greeting among Penangites is "Dah makan kah?" in Malay, "Chiak pa boi" in Hokkien, and "Saptacha" in Tamil, all of which translate to "Have you eaten?".

What are Penang's national holidays and major celebrations?

Penang celebrates the national holidays of Malaysia, which include:

1. New Year's Day (1st January): Celebrated as the beginning of the Gregorian calendar year, it is a public holiday in Penang and throughout Malaysia.

2. Chinese New Year: This is a significant celebration for the Chinese community in Penang. The date varies each year but usually falls in January or February. It is marked by vibrant decorations, lion and dragon dances, fireworks, and family gatherings.

3. Hari Raya Aidilfitri: Also known as Eid al-Fitr, this Muslim festival marks the end of Ramadan, the Islamic holy month of fasting. It is a joyous celebration of feasting, visiting relatives, and seeking forgiveness. The date varies based on the Islamic lunar calendar.

4. Hari Merdeka (Independence Day - 31st August): This national holiday commemorates Malaysia's independence from British colonial rule in 1957. Celebrations include parades, cultural performances, and patriotic events.

5. Malaysia Day (16th September): Malaysia Day commemorates the formation of Malaysia in 1963 when Peninsular Malaysia, Sabah, Sarawak, and Singapore joined together (Singapore later left the federation). It is a day to celebrate the unity and diversity of the country.

In addition to these national holidays, Penang also observes major cultural and religious celebrations such as Thaipusam (Hindu festival), Wesak Day (Buddhist festival), and Deepavali (Festival of Lights). These celebrations are marked by religious ceremonies, processions, and cultural performances.

It's important to note that dates for specific festivals may vary each year based on the lunar calendar, so it's advisable to check the exact dates for these celebrations when planning your visit to Penang.

Important travel safety information


Is Penang Safe?

Penang is generally considered a safe destination for travelers with a low crime rate. It's important to stay alert, secure your valuables, and exercise caution in crowded areas. Use licensed transportation options and be mindful of water safety. Follow food hygiene practices and drink bottled water. Stay informed about local customs and laws. While Penang is safe, it's always wise to exercise common sense and take necessary precautions to ensure personal safety while traveling.

What is the emergency number?

The emergency number in Penang, as well as throughout Malaysia, is 999. This number can be dialed for immediate assistance in case of emergencies such as accidents, crimes, or medical emergencies. When calling the emergency number, provide clear information about the situation and your location to help responders provide appropriate assistance.
