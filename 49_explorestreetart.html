Where to Find Street Art In Penang: 10 Murals, Map + Tour!

Wondering where to find Penang street art? This self-guided walking tour will help you find the best urban murals in George Town!



1. ‘Brother and Sister on a Swing’, Step By Step Lane (2012)


Step by Step Lane, located between the waterfront and town in George Town, is home to an iconic yet somewhat hidden street art. The lane was previously nameless until it gained recognition due to a particular artwork. Known as "Brother and Sister on a Swing," this interactive mural was created by Louis Gan, a self-taught artist who is deaf-mute. Gan specializes in watercolour, acrylic, and wall murals. Unlike some murals that convey complex messages, this artwork simply celebrates the bond between siblings, making it an endearing and charming piece of street art in George Town.



63 Gat Lebuh Chulia






2. ‘Old Soy Milk Stall’, Step by Step Lane


Adjacent to the "Brother and Sister on a Swing" mural, you will discover a delightful artwork depicting an elderly woman offering bowls of soy milk from a food cart. This mural, a collaboration between St. Xavier's Institution, Homesoy, and Vilmedia, combines the efforts of a school, a soy milk company, and an advertising agency. Although it serves as an advertisement, it is undeniably visually appealing and carries an aesthetic charm.



63 Gat Lebuh Chulia






3. ‘Love Me Like Your Fortune Cat’, Armenian Street


A heartwarming tale emerges from Penang, where street artists joined forces for the 101 Lost Kittens project. This initiative aimed to find homes for stray animals. Among the highlights of this endeavor are 12 cat-themed wall murals scattered throughout George Town.



28 Gat Lebuh Armenian






4. I Want Bao, Armenian Street (2014)


Created by Penang street artist, WK Setor, this mural shows two kids reaching for bao buns. Like the soy milk piece, it’s a clever piece of advertising commissioned by Ming Xiang Tai Pastry Shop beside it.



28 Gat Lebuh Armenian






5. ‘Little Children on a Bicycle’, Armenian Street (2012)


Enter Ernest Zacharevic! This piece of Penang street art on Armenian Street is one of his most famous, completed in 2012 for the George Town Festival. Apparently these cute kids are modelled on real children who were at an event with their parents when they began playing with Zacharevic’s bicycle. He was inspired and the rest is history!



2, Lebuh Armenian






6. ‘The Three Girls’, Armenian Street (2013)


Although it may not be apparent in the photo, this mural is relatively small in size. As you explore Lebuh Armenian and admire the various artworks in the area, be sure to keep a close eye out for this particular mural among the others on the street.



78, Lebuh Armenian






7. Reaching Up / Boy on a Chair, Cannon Street (2012)


This piece is another piece of Ernest Zacharevic street art in Penang. It’s an original, painted in 2012 but there’s a rumour it received a touch-up in 2016. It suddenly appeared brighter and more defined one day so it’s likely that Zach returned to nurture his work.



14 Lebuh Cannon






8. ‘Boy on a Motorbike’, Lebuh Ah Quee (2012)


Boy on a Motorbike is another Ernest Zacharevic creation. It’s been here since 2012 and its number of visitors grows each year.



12, Lebuh Ah Quee






9. ‘Boy on a Motorbike’, Lebuh Ah Quee (2012)


Situated in Little India, this artwork resides in an area influenced by the Indian community. Unlike bao buns, which were brought to Malaysia by Chinese settlers, roti is a popular food among the Indian community who arrived during the British colonial era. Currently, the Indian community comprises approximately 7% of the country's population.



55, Lebuh Armenian






10. ‘The Indian Boatman’, Lorong Stewart (2014)


Located in a back street slightly outside the town, specifically at the intersection of Lorong Stewart and Klang Street, this street art in Penang was crafted by Julia Volchkova, a renowned Russian artist recognized for her ability to capture human emotions and expressions through her artwork.



75, Lorong Stewart
