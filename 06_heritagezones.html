Heritage Zones

The George Town UNESCO World Heritage Site showcases meticulously restored traditional architecture and a vibrant array of heritage trades and arts, reflecting the multicultural influences that have shaped Penang. Spanning just 2.5 square kilometers, this compact area known as the 'Street of Harmony' invites exploration on foot, bicycle, or trishaw.


Georgetown’s Outstanding Universal Values (OUVs)

UNESCO recognizes the city as an extraordinary model of a multicultural trading town in Southeast Asia, born out of the cultural and commercial interactions between Malays, Chinese, Indians, and Europeans.


Core Zone (109.38 hectares)

The Historic City of George Town encompasses an area of 109.38 hectares, located on the northeastern cape of Penang Island, bordered by the Straits of Malacca, Lorong Love, Gat Lebuh Melayu, Lorong Carnarvon, and Lebuh Carnarvon. Within this core zone, there are over 1,700 historical buildings situated along four main streets—Pengkalan Weld, Lebuh Pantai, Jalan Masjid Kapitan Keling, and Lorong Love—as well as several perpendicular streets, including Jalan Tun Syed Sheh Barakbah, Lebuh Light, Lebuh Bishop, Lebuh Gereja, Lebuh China, Lebuh Pasar, Lebuh Chulia, Lebuh Armenian, and Lebuh Acheh.

Buffer Zone (150.04 hectares)

The Core Zone of the Historic City of George Town is safeguarded by a 150.04-hectare Buffer Zone (excluding the sea buffer), which is delimited by the sea area surrounding the harbor, Jalan Dr Lim Chwee Leong, and Gat Jalan Prangin in the southwest corner, as well as Jalan Transfer in the northwest corner.


Landmarks

Regarded as one of Malaysia's most renowned tourist destinations, Penang captures the attention of visitors with its captivating blend of culture, art, cuisine, and natural beauty, creating a unique sensory experience that sets it apart from other places.


Experience its mouth-watering cuisine, breathtaking heritage architecture, quirky art spots and relaxing beaches. Penang is a feast in many ways for any visitor who is looking to be awed by the authentic taste of Asia.

Museums & Art Galleries

Penang boasts a wealth of museums, showcasing its rich heritage and allowing visitors to delve into the captivating history that shaped the diverse cultural tapestry of the region. In addition to traditional museums, Penang is adorned with meticulously preserved heritage residences and clan houses, each serving as invaluable historical treasures.

While Penang's street art is widely celebrated, the state's art scene extends far beyond that, inviting exploration of numerous art galleries that showcase the flourishing artistic expressions inspired by life in the vibrant Pearl of the Orient. The compact size of Penang makes it effortlessly convenient to navigate between attractions on foot or by bicycle, ensuring easy access to the diverse array of experiences it has to offer.
