Fort Cornwallis


Fort Cornwallis is a historical fortress located in George Town, the capital city of Penang state in Malaysia. It is situated at the northeastern tip of Penang Island, overlooking the scenic views of the Malacca Strait. The fort was built by the British East India Company in the late 18th century, specifically in 1786, when Captain Francis Light established a British settlement in Penang.


The purpose of constructing Fort Cornwallis was to serve as a defensive structure against potential invasions and to protect the newly established British trading post. The fort was named after Charles Cornwallis, the Governor-General of Bengal, who played a significant role in the British colonization of India.


Fort Cornwallis features a star-shaped design, which was a common architectural style for military fortifications during that era. The fort's walls are made of bricks and are strategically built with bastions and cannons facing the sea and the mainland. It covers an area of approximately 4.5 hectares (11 acres).


Over the years, Fort Cornwallis has undergone several renovations and modifications, but it still retains its historical charm and significance. Within the fort, there are various structures and attractions, including a central parade ground, a chapel, a gunpowder magazine, and a historical lighthouse. Visitors can explore the fort and learn about its history through informative displays, artifacts, and guided tours.


oday, Fort Cornwallis is not only a popular tourist attraction but also serves as a venue for cultural events and exhibitions. It provides visitors with a glimpse into Penang's colonial past and offers panoramic views of the surrounding area. The fort stands as a testament to Penang's historical importance as a strategic trading port and symbolizes the British influence in the region.

Frequently Asked Questions


How do I get to Fort Cornwallis?

To get to Fort Cornwallis in George Town, Penang, you can walk from Komtar bus terminal in approximately 15-20 minutes or take a short taxi or ride-sharing service directly to the fort. If you're driving, you can use GPS to navigate to Jalan Tun Syed Sheh Barakbah. Parking is available nearby, but it may be limited.

What can I see at Fort Cornwallis??

At Fort Cornwallis, you can explore the historical fortification itself, which includes various structures such as the star-shaped fort, the historic entrance gate, and old cannons. There are also informational plaques and displays that provide insights into the history of the fort and the colonial era.

Are there guided tours available at Fort Cornwallis?

Yes, guided tours are available at Fort Cornwallis. Trained guides provide informative explanations about the history, architecture, and significance of the fort. They can also answer any questions you may have.

Can I take photographs at Fort Cornwallis?

Yes, photography is allowed at Fort Cornwallis. You are free to capture the fort's architecture, cannons, and surrounding areas. However, some areas or exhibits may have restrictions on photography, so it's best to respect any signage or instructions provided.

Are there any entrance fees to visit Fort Cornwallis?

Yes, there is an entrance fee to visit Fort Cornwallis. The fees are vary for different age groups, and there may be discounts for students or local residents.
