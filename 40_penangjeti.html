Penang's Jeti Lama Heritage Enclave
Penang's Jeti Lama Heritage Enclave

Butterworth, also known as Bagan, is an ideal spot for a peaceful getaway. Once a busy port, it offers an alternative to the bustling crowds of George Town. Accessible by a 15-minute ferry ride, Butterworth is undergoing urban regeneration efforts to revitalize the area and attract visitors. Jalan Jeti Lama, the town's historic foundation, has been revived as part of these initiatives.


Exploring Jalan Jeti Lama on foot allows visitors to uncover the old town's heritage sites, including the notable Sree Maha Mariamman Temple. This temple is a must-visit, adorned with a grand gopuram (tall entrance tower) and vibrant exterior, creating a captivating sight for visitors as they venture further along the road.


The Sree Maha Mariamman Temple serves as a place of worship for the South Indian community residing in the vicinity, particularly in Kampung Benggali. In the past, when Hindus were engaged in port activities, they would visit the temple to offer prayers to the mother deity Amman, who is revered as the Hindu goddess of rain. For workers who were separated from their families in India, the temple provided solace and a sense of connection. It's important to note that the temple typically closes in the afternoon and reopens at 5:30 PM.


The tarmac in front of the Sree Maha Mariamman Temple has been transformed into a functional parking lot and pocket garden, known as the Jeti Lama Rain Garden. This garden includes a raised stage, a pond with water plants, benches, and lights, along with an interactive water play feature. It serves as a vibrant venue for events, including the annual Butterworth Fringe Festival. Nearby, the Jeti Lama Market offers a variety of stalls selling fishing equipment, sundry items, vegetables, and fish. The area also features picturesque pre-war shophouses, making it a captivating destination for photographers and history enthusiasts. Jalan Jeti Lama showcases the efforts to highlight Butterworth's colorful past.
