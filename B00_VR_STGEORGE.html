St. George's Anglican Church

       St. George's Church in Penang, Malaysia, stands as a testament to the region's rich history. Established in 1818 by the British East India Company, it is the oldest Anglican church in Southeast Asia. With its elegant Georgian architecture and a towering steeple, the church exudes a sense of timeless beauty. It serves as a spiritual sanctuary and a cultural landmark, symbolizing the enduring legacy of Penang's colonial past.
