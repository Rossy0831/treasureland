A “Living Heritage Treasure of Penang”
A “Living Heritage Treasure of Penang”

Mr. Sim Buck Teik, the owner of Seang Hin Leong on Beach Street, holds the esteemed title of "Living Heritage Treasure of Penang." Although he is now in his eighties and no longer works full time, he still spends most of his day at the shop. Buck Teik takes pleasure in sitting in his shop, facing the street and enjoying the refreshing breeze. His son, Mr. Sim Hin Leong, and daughter, Ms. Sim Jeck Choo, continue the family business, ensuring its continuity. Buck Teik acquired his craft skills from his father in Teochew, China, before the family relocated to Malaya (the former name for Malaysia).


At Seang Hin Leong, the Sim family specializes in working with rattan and bamboo, crafting them into furniture and household objects. They possess exceptional skills in rattan weaving, capable of creating custom-made items and repairing old objects. In addition to selling their own handmade products, they also offer rattan weaving classes for tourists at a reasonable price.


While they source some ready-made products from China, Indonesia, and Singapore, the Sims remain dedicated to growing their business and maintaining their passion for rattan weaving. Most of their rattan supplies come from Pahang. Rattan is renowned for its durability, but proper treatment is crucial to prevent fragility. Before weaving, the rattan is soaked in water to enhance flexibility and prevent breakage. As it dries, it becomes tighter and sturdier. Buck Teik, with his expertise, ensures the smoothness of the rattan by using a knife and his hands to verify its texture. The Sim family creates a wide range of products, from furniture to unique items like rattan handbags. The time required for completion varies depending on the size, with smaller decorative items taking up to a day and furniture requiring several days..


The majority of customers at Seang Hin Leong are local residents. The shop follows fixed pricing for their products, with each item having a price tag attached. In addition to rattan products, they also sell souvenirs such as keychains, fridge magnets, and wooden spoons.


Having operated his business in this location for many years, Buck Teik appreciates the peaceful environment and is grateful that the road has maintained a quiet pace, allowing customers to easily find parking when visiting his shop. Above all, he is immensely proud that his children have inherited his passion for rattan weaving and are diligently working to expand and carry forward his cherished venture.
