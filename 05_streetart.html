Street Art

Looking to visit the street art? Here the list of all street art in Penang.

Wall Murals

During its time as a free port, Penang became a vibrant cosmopolitan city, enriched by the cultural exchange brought by international merchants. Today, Penang thrives as a hub for art, particularly its street art scene.


In 2012, Lithuanian artist Ernest Zacharevic's commissioned murals sparked a transformation, gaining global recognition and turning George Town's walls into captivating and interactive artworks. The street art scene in George Town has evolved remarkably, attracting art enthusiasts and visitors seeking a unique cultural experience.

The recognition of certain iconic wall murals in Penang has elevated them to the status of cultural landmarks, comparable to well-known attractions like the KOMTAR building and Penang Ferry. This artistic movement has provided opportunities for both local and international artists, inspiring a wave of creativity to flourish on the walls of buildings throughout Penang.

Urban Exchange

Urban Xchange has effectively expanded people's understanding of public art by fostering collaboration and dialogue among diverse artists from different continents, disciplines, and generations. Through this project, artists have been able to create boundary-pushing pieces that demonstrate the transformative power of exchanging ideas and perspectives.


The results are artworks that are unexpected, thrilling, and thought-provoking, showcasing the immense potential of art when given the opportunity to thrive through open exchange and collaboration.

Wire Sculptures

Explore George Town and keep an eye out for the whimsical and playful wire sculptures that add a touch of cheekiness to the city. In 2009, the Penang State Government initiated a project to commission 52 wire sculptures with distinct designs, strategically placed throughout the city. These sculptures not only serve as artistic installations but also share intriguing historical anecdotes that go beyond what you would typically find in traditional guidebooks. So, while exploring George Town, keep an eye out for these unique sculptures that bring a delightful blend of art and lesser-known historical facts to the streets.

Butterworth Art Walk

Although the mural scene in Seberang Perai developed later than in George Town, it is rapidly gaining momentum and catching up in terms of iconic street art. The Butterworth Art Walk stands as a testament to the vibrant artistic community in Penang and the region's rich historical background. It showcases the collaborative efforts of local artists and pays homage to the cultural heritage of the state.


Through its captivating murals and installations, the Butterworth Art Walk strives to provide visitors with an educational experience about the intriguing history of Seberang Perai. As you stroll along the small alley in Jalan Bagan Luar, you can discover fascinating facts about the region's agricultural economy and its unique history of elephant exports. It's a delightful surprise to find that history can serve as a quirky and interesting backdrop for memorable photo opportunities. The Art Walk combines art and history to create an engaging and immersive experience for all who visit.
