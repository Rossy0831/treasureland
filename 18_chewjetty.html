Chew Jetty


Chew Jetty Penang is located at Weld Quay, George Town, Penang and is 21 kilometres away from Lexis Suites Penang. It is the biggest of the waterfront settlements in Georgetown and was part of the Clan Jetties Penang settling at the southern section of Weld Quay. The wooden piers built by the Chinese were divided between the different surname-based clans such as “Lim”, “Tan”, “Lee”, “Yeoh” and finally “Chew”, which was the most famous of all. Chew Jetty has also become one of the focal points of the Chinese New Year celebrations in Penang in recent years.


Chew Jetty is unique because it has been preserved as a living heritage site. The houses on the jetty are built on stilts above the water, and the entire settlement stretches out over the sea. It offers visitors a glimpse into the traditional lifestyle and architecture of the Chinese community.


Today, Chew Jetty is a popular tourist attraction, drawing both locals and international visitors. It has a vibrant atmosphere with various shops, food stalls, and souvenir stores lining the wooden walkways. You can explore the jetty, enjoy the views of the sea and George Town's skyline, and experience the local culture.


It's important to note that Chew Jetty is a residential area, and people still live there, so it's crucial to be respectful of their privacy and follow any guidelines or rules set by the residents.


Visiting Chew Jetty provides a unique opportunity to learn about the historical and cultural significance of the Chinese community in Penang while enjoying the charming ambiance of this waterfront settlement.

Frequently Asked Questions


When is Chew Jetty open?

Chew Jetty is open daily from 9.00am to 9.00pm.

How do I get to Chew Jetty?

You can walk to Chew Jetty from the Weld Quay Ferry & Bus Terminal, a distance of 250 meters (800 ft). From the bus terminal, walk south along Pengkalan Weld until you reach Chew Jetty (with posted signboard) on your left.

Are there any rules or guidelines I should follow while visiting Chew Jetty?

Yes, it's important to be respectful to the residents and their properties. Avoid touching or entering private homes unless explicitly invited. Additionally, be mindful of your surroundings and avoid littering.

What can I expect to see at Chew Jetty?

Chew Jetty is a stilt village built on the water. When you visit, you can witness traditional wooden houses, walk along the wooden boardwalks, and observe the daily activities of the residents. There are also small shops selling souvenirs and local snacks.

Is there an entrance fee to visit Chew Jetty?

No, there is no entrance fee to visit Chew Jetty. It is open to the public, and you can freely explore the area.
