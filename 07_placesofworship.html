Places Of Worship

As a trading port for the British East India Company, Penang attracted a diverse array of workers and traders from around the globe, contributing to its multicultural fabric. Alongside the dominant Chinese, Malay, and Indian communities, Penang is also home to significant Eurasian and Siamese communities, each bringing their distinct beliefs, customs, and traditions to the island.


Penang has maintained a longstanding tradition of religious tolerance, evident in the presence of diverse places of worship, often located in close proximity to one another. A prime illustration of this is Jalan Masjid Kapitan Keling, popularly known as the Street of Harmony, where the Kapitan Keling Mosque, Goddess of Mercy Temple, St. George's Church, and Sri Maha Mariamman Temple coexist harmoniously within a short walking distance.
